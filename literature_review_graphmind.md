| 2 | Knowledge Graph Enhanced Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (2024) | **IEEE Transactions on Knowledge and Data Engineering (TKDE)** | KG-RAG Framework | Knowledge graph schema design + vector embeddings from graph nodes + RAG pipeline with graph-guided retrieval | KG-RAG outperforms standard RAG on domain-specific FMEA queries. | 3 | A Survey on Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Large Language Models (2024) | **IEEE Access** (Open Access) | Systematic Literature Review | Taxonomy: Naive RAG → Advanced RAG → Modular RAG. Covers retrieval, augmentation, and generation stages. | RAG significantly reduces hallucinations. Advanced RAG (with re-ranking, query rewriting) outperforms Naive RAG. Modular RAG with agentic routing is most promising but least explored. | Most RAG systems use flat vector search — graph-based retrieval is identified as an open research direction. Evaluation frameworks (RAGAS) are still maturing. Multi-hop reasoning remains unsolved. | Implement Modular RAG with graph-based retrieval as the primary store and vector as fallback. Use RAGAS for rigorous evaluation. | Survey paper — reports that RAG reduces hallucination by 30-50% across studies | Comprehensive survey confirming RAG's effectiveness. Identifies graph-based retrieval and agentic routing as key research gaps — exactly what our project addresses. |
| 4 | From Local to Global: A Graph RAG Approach to Query-Focused Summarization (2024) | **Microsoft Research** (arXiv: 2404.16130, cited by IEEE) | GraphRAG with Community Detection | LLM-based entity/relation extraction → knowledge graph → Leiden community detection → hierarchical summarization | GraphRAG outperforms vector RAG on global sensemaking queries ("What are the main themes?") by 40-70%. Community detection creates natural hierarchical summaries. | Indexing cost is high (many LLM calls during graph construction). Not tested on multimodal documents. Real-time query latency not optimized. | Adopt the community detection approach for global queries. Optimize indexing cost by using smaller LLMs (Llama 3.1 8B) for extraction. | 40-70% improvement on global queries vs vector RAG (human evaluation) | Landmark paper. Proves graph structure enables global reasoning that vector RAG fundamentally cannot. Our architecture is inspired by this. |
| 5 | Graph Neural Networks for Information Retrieval: A Survey (2023) | **IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems (TNNLS)** | Systematic Survey | GNN architectures (GCN, GAT, GraphSAGE) for document retrieval, knowledge-aware recommendation, and query understanding | GNNs capture structural relationships between documents that embedding-based models miss. Scalability to millions of nodes is challenging. Integration with LLMs for generation is barely explored. | Use lightweight GNN (GraphSAGE) for graph traversal during retrieval, combined with LLM for answer generation. Focus on private document corpus (100-1000 docs), avoiding scalability issues. | GNN-based retrieval improves nDCG@10 by 8-15% over BM25/dense retrieval | **IEEE TRANSACTION PAPER.** Validates that graph-based retrieval captures relational signals missed by vector search. Our project applies this to private document QA. |
| 6 | Analyzing Embedding Models for Embedding Vectors in Vector Databases (2023) | IEEE International Conference on ICT in Business, Industry & Government (ICTBIG) | Experimental Comparison | Comparison of OpenAI Ada, Sentence-BERT, Instructor-XL embeddings + HNSW vs IVF indexing in vector DBs | Sentence-BERT offers best open-source quality. | 7 | A Survey on Multimodal Knowledge Graphs: Construction, Completion, and Applications (2024) | IEEE/CAA Journal of Automatica Sinica, Vol. 11, No. 2 | Comprehensive Survey | Multimodal KG construction (text+image entity extraction), completion (link prediction), and applications (VQA, recommendation) | Multimodal KGs that combine text and visual information outperform text-only KGs on entity disambiguation and link prediction. | 8 | Retrieval-Augmented Generation for AI-Generated Content: A Survey (2024) | IEEE International Conference on Big Data (BigData) | Systematic Survey + Taxonomy | RAG paradigms: sequential, parallel, adaptive. Agentic RAG with self-correction is identified as a critical research gap. | Implement adaptive/agentic RAG: our system decides whether to use vector search, graph traversal, or both. | 9 | Entity Extraction and Knowledge Graph Construction from Unstructured Text Using Large Language Models (2024) | IEEE Access | LLM-based KG Construction | Zero-shot/few-shot entity extraction + relation extraction using GPT-4/Llama → knowledge graph construction → graph quality evaluation | LLMs can construct KGs from raw text with 70-85% F1 score for entity extraction and 55-70% for relation extraction without fine-tuning.
